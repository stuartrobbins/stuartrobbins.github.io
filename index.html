Is College Worth it?

Making the right decision with proper evidence

With student debt totalling reaching over 1 trillion dollars even higher than credit card debt, there is reasonable doubt with people's ability to go to college. Taking out expensive loans is not for everyone, and depending on circumstances and their desires, the argument can be presented that other options can be the best possible decision.

Student Debt

The cost of higher education has been increasing in recent years, not only has the amount of students taking out student loans increased due to this, but the amount of debt per student has also gone up. Another effect of slow income growth is a higher “cohort default rate” in which a group of students default on their repayment of student loans (Glater 2015). This means that more and more college students are unable to repay their student loans

Loan education is lacking which causes students to be locked into high interest rate loans that eat away at them for years. When taking out student loans, many prospective college students do not completely understand the terms of the loans they are taking out to pay for their higher education. Currently, informed access to student loans include entrance and exit counseling that are required to be available to student borrowers. Before signing the promissory note to acquire a loan, borrows must complete an interactive quiz that does not require a high enough level of knowledge to pass.

To achieve more transparency about student loans and help students make informed decisions about their financial futures, institutions of higher education should provide in-person counseling and also provide in-person counseling throughout the time between the required entrance and exit counseling. In-person counseling sessions can more accurately determine whether a borrower has adequate knowledge of loan information than an online quiz.

The Value of College

While the benefits in academics and financial well being after college are well documented and known, there are more implicit reasons to also consider. The connections and experience of college in general is recognized as an incubator for developing social skills

Why are college students typically more well rounded?

For starters, college admission requires significant self-awareness and planning, as students go through the preparation for specialized tests and select schools that fit their academic and personal aspirations.The process of applying to college also involves demonstrating personal qualities through essays and extracurricular activities. Once enrolled, students face diverse social and academic environments that help them develop skills such as empathy, emotional stability, and effective communication with a wide array of peers and faculty. Through these phases of college students become more well rounded individuals.

How does it affect economic mobility?

According to the Brookings Institution, a think tank that conducts research in economics, college grads are more likely to make it up the economic ladder than those who do not graduate. Brookings cites research (image above) that found that 47% of children born into the lower economic quantile are likely to stay in that quantile if they do not get a degree which is four times more than those that get a four-year degree (10%). Essentially, 90% of those in the lower quartile that got a degree were able to move up to a higher quantile. As the quantile increases, the percentage of those that stay in the quantile who did not get a degree steadily decreases, as they fall to a lower quantile. Meanwhile, those with a degree tended to have a higher percentage in staying in those higher quantiles.
